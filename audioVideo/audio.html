The <audio> t ag is used to embed sound content in a document, such as music or other audio streams.

The <audio> tag contains one or more <srouce> tags with different audio sources. The browser will choose the first source it supports.

The text between the <audio> and </audio> tags will only be displayed in browser that do not support the <audio> element.

There are three supported audio formats in HTML: MP3, WAV, and OGG.

        <!DOCTYPE html>
        <html>
            <head>
                <meta charset="UTF-8">
                <meta http-equiv="X-UA-Compatible" content="IE=edge">
                <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
            </head>
            <body>
            <audio controls>
                <source src="horse.ogg" type="audio/ogg">
                <source src="horse.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">
                Your browser does not support the audio tag.
            </audio>        
            </body> 
        </html>
