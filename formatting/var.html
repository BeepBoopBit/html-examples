The <var> tag is used to defines a variable in programming or in a mathematical expression. The content inside is typically displayed in italic.

Tip: This tag is not deprecated. However, it is possible to achieve richer effect by using CSS.

The area of a triangle is: 1/2 x h, where b is the base, and h is the vertical height.

        <!DOCTYPE html>
        <html>
            <head>
                <meta charset="UTF-8">
                <meta http-equiv="X-UA-Compatible" content="IE=edge">
                <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
            </head>
            <body>
                <p>The area of a triangle is: 1/2 x <var>h</var>, where <var>b</var> is the base, and <var>h</var> is the vertical height.</p>
            </body> 
        </html>
