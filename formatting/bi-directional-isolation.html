The <bdi> tag stands for bi-directional isolation.

This tag isolates a part of the text from being formatted in a different direction.

Note: The <bdi> and <bdo> tags are often mistakenly interchanged. Remember that <bdi> is for
isolating a text from being formatted to a certain direction while <bdo> formats the orientation
of a text whether from a left to right orientation (ltr) or to a right to left orientation (rlt).

See <bdo> for more explanation and examples.

Example:

        <!DOCTYPE html>
        <html>
            <head>
                <meta charset="UTF-8">
                <meta http-equiv="X-UA-Compatible" content="IE=edge">
                <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
                <title>bdi</title>
            </head>
            <body>
                <p> <bdo dir="rtl">The <bdi>quick brown fox</bdi> jumps over the lazy dog.</p>
            </body>
        </html>
