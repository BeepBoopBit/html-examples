The <ruby> tag is for ruby annotations.

Ruby characters are small, annotative characters placed above other text characters usually from
the Japanese "Kanji", Korean "Hanja", and Chinese "Hanzi".

Use <ruby> together with <rt> and <rp>: The <ruby> element consists of one or more characters that
needs an explanation/pronunciation, and an <rt> element that gives that information, and an
optional <rp> element that defines what to show for browsers that do not support ruby
annotations.

Example:

        <!DOCTYPE html>
            <html>
                <head>
                    <meta charset="UTF-8">
                    <meta http-equiv="X-UA-Compatible" content="IE=edge">
                    <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
                    <title>Ruby</title>
                </head>
                <body>
                    <ruby>
                        漢 <rt> ㄏㄢˋ </rt>
                        </ruby>
                </body>
            </html>
